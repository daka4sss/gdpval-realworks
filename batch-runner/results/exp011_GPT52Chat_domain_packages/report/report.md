No external grading or validation was applied; all quality signals discussed here reflect the model’s internal assessment at runtime.

## Quality Analysis

Self-assessed QA scores clustered mostly in the mid-range (5–7), indicating moderate confidence in task outputs rather than strong certainty. Government tasks showed the highest average self-QA confidence (7.2), while Health Care and Social Assistance exhibited the lowest (5.6), suggesting increased difficulty or ambiguity in healthcare-related prompts. Information, Manufacturing, and Professional Services sectors all showed similar confidence levels around 5.8, pointing to stable but unexceptional perceived output quality.

Sectors with slightly lower completion rates, such as Wholesale Trade and Retail Trade, did not show proportionally lower self-QA confidence, implying that failures were more likely tied to execution or packaging issues rather than low-confidence reasoning. Deliverable file generation was generally successful given the high completion rate, but the modest self-QA averages indicate that outputs often met baseline requirements without strong internal assurance of optimal quality.
